A Summary of

The Effects of Housing Assistance on Labor Supply:
Evidence from a Voucher Lottery

By Brian A. Jacob and Jens Ludwig

Context

There are 3.6 million eviction cases filed each year in the U.S.

The negative outcomes associated with housing insecurity has triggered a public debate over policies that address evictions

Policy Tradeoffs

Right to Counsel
Provide tax-funded legal representation to tenants facing eviction cases

Rental Assistance

Open Claims

  • A longer eviction process reduces the likelihood that delinquent renters end up being evicted by providing tenants with more time to repay their debt
  • Item 2
  • Item 3

Key Claim

Because rent delinquencies are driven by persistent shocks to income, policies that make it harder to evict tend to extend the length of the eviction process but are overall ineffective in preventing evictions

Results

Under a rental subsidy, the average realized rent in the bottom segment is unchanged relative to the baseline economy

Results

  • Quarterly employment rates drop by 4 percentage points
  • Quarterly earnings drop by $329
  • TANF receipt increase by 2 percentage points
